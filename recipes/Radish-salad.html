Carrot Rice

Radishes are crisp and peppery, and when combined with a sweet, citrus-flavored dressing, everybody will be coming back for more of this refreshing salad. We enjoy it as a luncheon salad with crackers and garnished with hard-boiled egg wedges, but it's also the perfect sidekick for a spring/summer cookout or picnic.

Refrigerate: 30 mins

Total: 45 mins

Prep: 15 mins

Servings: 4

Yield: 4 servings

Ingredients

Dressing:

  • 2 tablespoons orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • ½ teaspoon white sugar
  • 1 clove garlic, peeled and crushed
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Dressing:

  • 2 cups thinly sliced radishes
  • ½ cup frozen petite peas, thawed
  • ¼ cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 2 hard-cooked eggs, cut into wedges

Directions

  1. Step

    2. Whisk orange juice, lime juice, olive oil, rice vinegar, sugar, garlic, salt, and pepper together in a bowl.

  2. Step

    3. Combine radishes, peas, and red onion in a large bowl. Drizzle dressing over the salad and give it a quick stir until well combined. If time permits, move the bowl to the refrigerator for 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  3. Step

    4. Serve garnished with hard-cooked egg wedges.

Directions

A mandolin works best for slicing the radishes and onion if you have it - it's quick and easy.

If you don't have rice vinegar, white wine vinegar is a good substitute.

Nutrition Facts

Per Serving: 90 calories; fat 6.9g; sodium 27.5mg; carbohydrates 6.3g; protein 1.3g.
